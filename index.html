Who Am I?

Hey guys I’m Michael Scaringi, I was born and raised in Melbourne. Completed secondary school then moved to Ballarat with my family, but quickly found out the gold rush ended 160 years ago, so I moved back down to Melbourne in a share house with a couple of friends. I have a strong Italian background, can partially speak Italian and I can make a pasta sauce that mia mamma can be proud of. An interesting fact about me is that as a child I had an original Macintosh that my uncle gave to me on my birthday.

Also I think space is pretty neat.

Why I.T?

My interest in I.T started last year when I made revolutionary YouTube videos about the monthly Mario kart tournaments that I have with friends. The fun started when I legally downloaded apps such as Photoshop, Premier pro and After effects, I really enjoyed playing around, experimenting and creating content with those apps. So what does this have to do with I.T? good question, while using these apps are fun and rewarding in their own way, I believe to develop and better understand what I’m doing and how I’m able to do it with a degree in I.T will give me a deeper understanding and help better my content in the future.

Why RMIT?

Me and RMIT go way back, back to 2013 in fact, as a doey eyed teenager fresh out of high school I was unprepared for the amount of work an engineering degree would have and that I shouldn’t have chosen this degree simply because I was GoOd aT mATh. So my main reason for joining RMIT a second time is to not repeat the same mistake as the first time, do a degree I have a vested interest in and prove to myself that I am capable of achieving a degree in higher education.

What i want to learn at RMIT

What I want with this degree is to fundamentally learn why I can do the things I do on a computer/phone and use that to create more content. You’ve used content twice but what do you mean by it? Another good question; Like IT, the content is very broad and can mean a number of things and that’s, I want to develop apps, create games make videos, learn to code. I believe the benefit of doing an I.T degree is that you can study many different areas of I.T and eventually refine that knowledge into a career of my choice and continue in some aspects of I.T as an interesting hobby.
